INTRODUCTION

  • The agriculture sector accounts for 18.9% of Pakistanâ€™s GDP, takes up 47% of the total land area and 61% of rural community make their earnings directly or indirectly from agriculture and has a significant impact on the economy of the country.

  • Every year Pakistan faces a huge loss in the agriculture sector due to several factors such as extreme climate variations, flooding, lack of technology adoption and improper use of major resources like water, fertilizer and pesticides. These problems can be overcome by smart farming. Smart farming is a modern farming concept which utilizes IoT technology that is built for monitoring the crop field with the help of sensors and automating the irrigation system.

  • Coupled with the development of Geographic Information Systems (GIS) it will provide a powerful solution for analyzing crop status, health and help farmers identify high and low yielding areas on their farms. It includes the usage of geolocations, GPS tracking and interconnected sensors to monitor the fields and crops. To make the Smart Farming more sustainable, reliable and productive we will employ machine learning and data analytics that can make recommendations on the use of input data in real time.
